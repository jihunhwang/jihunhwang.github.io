**Nonlinear Waves**

Everyone would admit that linear equations are much easier to deal with than nonlinear ones. However, our nature is rather nonlinear, hence complicated but interesting. The most famous nonlinear equation (PDE), in my experience, would be the Navier-Stokes equation. Equivalent to Newton's 2nd law of motion, it describes the motion of an incompressible, viscous and homogeneous fluid in space. The existence of smooth, analytic solutions to the Navier-Stokes equation in 3-dimensional space is yet unknown. See [Navier-Stokes existence and smoothness problem](https://www.claymath.org/sites/default/files/navierstokes.pdf) for the precise statement/overview of the problem. This problem is very special to me, as it is what led me to major in mathematics at university when I was in middle school.

Hence, it is crucial to understand nonlinear dynamics in order to solve the mysteries of our nature. Indeed, the Navier-Stokes equation is an equation among numerous equations and dynamical systems in the world!
