Fresh out of undergraduate, I have to confess that my research interest is a bit 'all over the place' and still smoothly (hence continuously) permuting. My (current) research interest can be summarized as "**Solving physics/computer science problems from mathematical standpoints**".

I am mainly interested in studying geometric analysis (geometric flows, general relativity, etc.), nonlinear waves (Draupner wave, Navier-Stokes, etc.), and theoretical machine learning (convex optimization, etc.) in graduate school. I also have a decent interest in cybersecurity.

Most importantly, I am always open to learn new fields! A colleague of mine ([Patrick Lei](https://people.umass.edu/plei/)) had recently introduced me to the world of geometric representation theory and algebraic geometry.
