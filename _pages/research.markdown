<p>
Fresh out of undergraduate, I have to confess that my research interest is a bit 'all over the place' and still smoothly (hence continuously) permuting. My interest can be summarized as "<b>Solving physics/computer science problems from mathematical standpoints</b>".
</p>
<p>
I am interested in studying differential geometry, mainly in <b>geometric analysis</b> (geometric flows, minimal and constant-mean-curvature surfaces, etc.), and the directly derived fields such as general relativity and integrable systems.
</p>
<p>
The true beauty of differential geometry, in my opinion, (quite) often appears in its applications. Information and computational geometry are great examples, but I find its links with other distinct fields little more fascinating, such as nonlinear dynamics (perturbation theory, Ergodic theory, etc.) and theoretical machine learning (convex optimization, dimensionality reduction etc.). I hope to narrow down my area of interest in or after graduate school.
</p>
<p>
Most importantly, I am always open to learn new fields! A colleague of mine (<a href="https://people.umass.edu/plei/">Patrick Lei</a>) had recently introduced me to the world of geometric representation theory and algebraic geometry.
</p>
